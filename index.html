Prometheus Overview

Prometheus is a teleoperation system designed to simplify the collection of high-quality datasets for training robots. It supports both single and bimanual tasks, using motion capture and force feedback to enable precise robotic manipulation.

Key Features
  • Motion Tracking with HTC Vive Trackers 2.0: Leverages affordable HTC Vive Trackers 2.0 to accurately track user movements, ensuring intuitive control of the robot.
  • Force Feedback Controller: A custom-built controller provides real-time tactile feedback, allowing users to sense the robot’s grip for improved precision.
  • Special Gripper Design: The Robotiq gripper features custom fingers that evenly distribute pressure across a force sensor, delivering reliable feedback.
Design and Accessibility

Prometheus is engineered for reliability, affordability, and ease of replication. Its use of accessible components and open-source designs makes it suitable for researchers, laboratories, and startups working on robot learning.

Adaptability

Currently optimized for the UR3 robotic arm, Prometheus has a flexible architecture that can be adapted to other robotic platforms, expanding its range of potential applications.

Proven Effectiveness

Experiments demonstrate that Prometheus effectively handles delicate objects, with force feedback contributing to precise control. These results show its value as a tool for supporting robotics research and dataset collection.

Prometheus offers a practical, cost-effective solution for advancing robotic systems, combining intuitive operation with adaptability for various research needs.
